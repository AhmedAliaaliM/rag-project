# Week 3 Findings — Chunking Strategy Comparison

## Strategies Tested

| Strategy | Chunk Size | Overlap | Total Chunks | Avg Length |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| v1_fixed_500 | 500 chars | 50 | 4354 | 429 chars |
| v2_sentence_300 | 300 chars | 30 | 8548 | 216 chars |
| v3_large_1000 | 1000 chars | 100 | 2319 | 839 chars |

## Results

| Strategy | Hit Rate | Avg Latency |
|---|---|---|
| v1_fixed_500 (baseline) | 20/20 (100%) | 40.5s |
| v2_sentence_300 | 20/20 (100%) | 28.6s |
| v3_large_1000 | 20/20 (100%) | 68.0s |

## Winner: v2_sentence_300

All three strategies achieved identical retrieval accuracy (100%).
Selection was based on latency — v2 is 36% faster than baseline
and 58% faster than v3.

## Why v2 is faster
Smaller chunks mean less text stuffed into each LLM prompt.
Less tokens to process = faster generation time.

## What we'd try next
- Semantic chunking (group by meaning, not size)
- Larger top_k retrieval (fetch 10 instead of 5)
- Hybrid search (keyword + semantic combined)
